MAGLEV MEMBER

I joined the Cornell Hyperloop team as a freshman and initially started out by working only on the Magnetic Levitation team working on a variety of tasks as can be seen below. Further through the year I began to work and help develop the team's website. During the summer I assumed the role of website team lead to help develop the website full time and work on various graphic developments.



Density vs Field Strength
Click to enlarge

My first year I worked on a variety of tasks on the Maglev team such as selecting the material necessary for the Hyperloop pod Halbach levitation arrays. I learned and used the CES EduPack program to help make graphs and comparisons between different materials to help choose the best magnet in relation to, field strength, mechanical properties, temperature resistance, weight and cost. The final decision can be read in the full 2017 Cornell Hyperloop Pod Design Package (insert reference here).

After working on material selection for a couple months I began to be trained in Ansys Maxwell the electromagnetic simulation software to help design and simulate the levitation and drag forces of the Halbach arrays for the Hyperloop pod. I spent a large amount time learning how to develop images and force diagrams. Lateral Control Test Rig

This year I took alternate duties because of the increase in funding. As a result I was put in charge of a test rig that could simulate the lateral and rotational motion that the pod’s lateral control would experience. I compiled a full parts order and developed CADs to design the test rig. To the left and below renders of the test rig can be seen. In the coming months the assembly will be constructed and used to test Maglev’s lateral control and Suspension teams rail follower.
