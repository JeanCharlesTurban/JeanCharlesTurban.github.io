CEO

Prometheus is a small startup I founded in early May 2017. It quickly shifted from an idea to a small startup after going through the Rev Hardware Accelerator and the formation of a team of young Cornell Engineers. I work on this company during the semester and on breaks moving towards the company's goal of launching our product. I design and head the products development on both hardware and software ends. The Prometheus Keyboard is an ultra customizable keyboard that helps users such as engineers, architects and graphic designers to work as efficiently as they want to by catering their keyboard to their work.



As a result of the small team size many of the members of the team work across the board in multiple areas of product development including me. Using my experience with programming in multiple languages I worked with my CTO in developing the initial software in Java which has well documented GUI classes which greatly aided our work.



PRODUCT

The main challenge in terms of hardware for this project has been designing an ergonomic design that remains functional yet visually appealing. In addition obviously learning the internal mechanics of the keyboard have provided some small challenges. Most of our work is done in Fusion and prototyping is done with rapid 3D printing and laser cutting.





On top of working in both the hardware and software realms of development I also actively lead the business development by looking for funding, competing in pitch competitions and aiding any way I could to the product development. The Business team also conducted extensive market research to help the product development team gain an understanding of what our customer segments desired in our product. I have conducted over hundreds of interviews with engineering, architecture and design firms to gain insight into this issue.
